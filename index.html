Privacy Policy — ObsCalc

Last updated: 17 June 2026

ObsCalc ("the app") is an offline obstetric calculator for clinicians. This policy explains what data is and is not handled when you use the app. By using ObsCalc you agree to this policy.

1. Information the app does not collect

ObsCalc does not ask for, collect, store, or transmit any patient data or personal health information. All calculations (gestational age, estimated due date, and related values) run entirely on your device. The dates and values you enter are not saved, uploaded, or shared, and are discarded when you leave the relevant screen.

ObsCalc does not require an account, login, name, email, or phone number. A name you may optionally enter for the greeting is stored only on your own device and is never transmitted.

2. Advertising (Google AdMob)

The free version of ObsCalc displays advertisements through Google AdMob. To serve ads, Google and its partners may access your device's advertising identifier (Advertising ID) and limited technical information such as device type, general location derived from IP address, and ad-interaction data. This is used to deliver and measure ads.

ObsCalc itself does not collect or store this information; it is handled by Google AdMob. Google's handling of this data is described in Google's Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy, and you can read how Google uses data from apps that use its services here: https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites.

You can reset or limit your Advertising ID at any time in your device settings (Settings → Google → Ads). Users in regions requiring consent (such as the EEA/UK) are shown a consent request before personalised ads are served.

3. Removing ads (in-app purchase)

ObsCalc offers a one-time in-app purchase ("Remove Ads Forever", ₹99) that removes all advertising. This purchase is processed by Google Play Billing. ObsCalc does not receive or store your payment details; payment is handled entirely by Google Play. The app stores only a local flag indicating that ads have been removed. Google's handling of purchase data is covered by Google's Privacy Policy linked above.

4. Data storage

The only data stored by ObsCalc is your app preferences (such as date format, cycle length, theme, optional greeting name, and whether ads have been removed). This is stored locally on your device only and is never transmitted to us or any third party.

5. Children

ObsCalc is a professional tool intended for clinicians and is not directed at children.

6. Medical disclaimer

ObsCalc is a calculation aid for qualified healthcare professionals. It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment, and must not replace clinical judgement. The developer is not responsible for clinical decisions made using the app.

7. Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date.
